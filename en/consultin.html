What do you need to know about audits? The Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) selectively audits tax returns and rebate claims to ensure that all returns and claims are properly prepared and that taxpayers receive all payments to which they are entitled.

What are your responsibilities? By law, you have to keep adequate books and records in order to determine your tax obligations and entitlements. Generally, books and records must be kept for a minimum of six years. If you maintain your accounting records on a computer, you must keep them in an electronically readable format, even if you also keep a paper record. Using the services of a tax professional does not relieve you of these responsibilities. In an audit, the CRA will ask you to make available to the auditor your books and records (both paper and electronic), as well as any supporting documents; you will also be expected to supply answers to the auditor’s questions.
