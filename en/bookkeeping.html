BOOKKEEPING

Bookkeeping is the recording of financial transactions, including sales, purchases, income, receipts, and payments made by an individual or an organization.

A bookkeeper, also known as an “accounting clerk” or “accounting technician,” is a person who records the day-to-day financial transactions of an organization.

The bookkeeper is usually responsible for writing the daybooks — recording purchases, sales, receipts, and payments. Furthermore, the bookkeeper will ensure that all transactions are recorded in the correct daybook, supplier ledger, customer ledger, or general ledger. The bookkeeper also brings the books to the trial balance stage.
